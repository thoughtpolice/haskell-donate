Security and privacy

Security and privacy are taken seriously when using the donation form. Donations rely on almost no outside services, and can be made (for the most part) completely anonymously. Here you can find some notes and common points addressing some questions you might have.

Source code

The source to this service is available on GitHub.

Your credit card details are never stored in any way, on any server we run. Your credit card details will never leave your browser. We use a temporary, one-time-use token that is generated in your browser, and have Stripe debit your account using that token.

The only identifiable information we have is the information you leave in the submission form, namely, your name and email address. This email address is ONLY used to send email receipts to you, and so we can do basic accounting of the donations. If multiple donations come from the same email address, the donation service recognizes these, and groups these together under one donor. This is the only aggregation or accounting that is done.

Email addresses are ONLY used for receipts, and for basic donor accounting. Receipts DO NOT contain any truly confidential information, they are for accounting purposes; you may use throw-away or burner email addresses.

This site is ONLY served over HTTPS. Strict-Transport-Security is enabled. HTTP/2 is enabled. SSL certificates use ECDSA signatures, with ECDHE as the key exchange mechanism. Certificates are signed and generated by Let's Encrypt, once every 30 days. SSL cipher suites are generally strong, including perfect-forward secrecy (e.g. AES-GCM or ChaCha20/Poly1305). No external services besides Stripe are used.

There is no true persistent database behind this application; the only record of donations and donors is maintained by Stripe internally.

You must have JavaScript enabled on this domain in order to donate.

There is (generally) no attempt to block or otherwise curb traffic from systems such as Tor. When combined with Bitcoin, and a throw away email, this gives you a decent level of anonymity and shielding.

There are no CDNs of any kind (such as Fastly or Cloudflare) used for this domain. The only files served from outside our domain are integrations with Stripe.

There are no third party JavaScript libraries used in this page other than Stripe.js and Checkout.js, and there is very minimal amounts of code in the donation server, in order to keep the codebase small, and have maximum auditability.

While we do not have any tracking, or use any 3rd party tracking services on this page, Stripe, by way of their JavaScript code, does use trackers like Mixpanel. Stripe doesn't support external usage of its JavaScript libraries at this time without these services. Thus, while we have no skin in the game — so to speak — the donation form as it exists today does have minor amounts of analytics/tracking.
